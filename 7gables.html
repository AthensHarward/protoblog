Short Bio
Born: July 4th, 1804
Died: May 19th, 1864
Birthplace: Salem, Massachusetts

Considered to be one the great American authors, Nathaniel Hawthorne wrote several novels that remain popular even today. The Scarlet Letter and The House of the Seven Gables being just two examples. Growing up in Salem, Hawthorne had a direct ancestor who had been a judge during the Salem witch trials in the town's history. Hawthorne himself mused whether the hardships of his family line since then were the result his ancestors actions. One of the women that had been sentenced to death by his ancestor had purportedly cursed the Hawthorne family. This plays along a similar theme with Pyncheon family curse in The House of the Seven Gables. 1

Published in 1851, The House of the Seven Gables was modeled after a real house owned by Hawthorne's cousin. The actual house was built in 1668 just 24 years before the Salem witch trials. The history of the house being related to the trials and Hawthorne's own family's role in the event, has left the house as one of the most haunted locations in Salem. The house is still standing today as a museum. 2

Chapter 10
The Pyncheon Garden

Today while walking and listening, Hawthorne gave a lot of space to describing the garden and the chickens. I have had several years experience being around gardens and chickens, so I am very familiar with this kind of setting. Hawthorne's description of the tiniest details and his ability to draw out the most nestalgic parts were absolutely incredible. It was like he was able to distill the essence of these experiences and give the reader the joy of this world.
For someone who has never experienced these things or has had bad or limited experiences, this is the perfect way to enter the soul of someone who truly finds peace in these things.
