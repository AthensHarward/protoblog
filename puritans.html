The Puritans

Intentions
The purpose of these articles is to give basic background information to help with the context of specific events or characters. It is not meant for an exhaustive study.

Common Stereotypes
I am mostly focused on a broad, bird's eye view of the movement, so I will not be spending much time exploring the truth of sterotypes.

Even though they existed within the Anglican Church of England. The people we are talking about were theologically descended from Calvinists, They were further divided into two groups, the Puritans and the Separatists. They were similar in many regards, but their greatest difference between them was their position on if the Church of England should be reformed or abandoned. (2) They remained as distinct groups even in the New World.

Whether or not most us are aware of the players, two well known roles of these movements in history were as the Pilgrims that landed at Plymouth Rock (Separatists) and as the instigators of the Salem Witch Trials (Puritans). As far as people's opinion of them, both past and present, they were known for being extremely prudish and morally strict. In fact, this is where we get the word Puritanical, which references their work ethic in addition to their disdain of pleasure.

Origins


The Protestant Reformation
    1517 - Martin Luther nails his 95 Thesis to the church door (according to tradition)
    1522 - Ulrich Zwingli openly challenges the Catholic Church
    1536 - John Calvin publishes Institutes of the Christian Religion

The English Reformation

    1534 - Henry the 8th passes the Act of Supremacy
    (This break from the Roman Church kept many of the Catholic traditions. This laid the seeds for the Puritans to rise up from within the Church of England.)
    1620s & 1630s - The Puritan Great Migration


The New World


First Settlements
    1620 - The pilgrims land at Plymouth Rock
    1626 - The Puritans establish Naumkeag/Salem, Massachusetts (It was renamed Salem in 1629)
Plymouth, Massachusetts: Established in 1620 by Separatists.

Salem, Massachusetts: Founded in 1626 by Puritans

Boston, Massachusetts: Founded in 1630 by Puritans seeking religious freedom

Hartford, Connecticut: Settled in 1636 by Puritans from Massachusetts

New Haven, Connecticut: Founded in 1638 by a group of Separatists


Harvard University: Founded in 1636 by Puritans
Yale University: Established in 1701 by Congregationalists (Descended from Puritanism)
