Common Stereotypes
I am mostly focused on a broad, bird's eye view of the movement, so I will not be spending much time exploring the truth of sterotypes.

The people we are talking about were Calvinists. They were further divided into two groups, the Puritans and the Separatists. They were the same people with the same beliefs. What distinguished them was their position on if the Church of England should be reformed or abandoned. (2)

Whether or not most us are aware of the players, two well known roles of these Calvinists in history were as the Pilgrims that landed at Plymouth Rock (Separatists) and as the instigators of the Salem Witch Trials (Puritans). As far as people's opinion of them, both past and present, they were known for being extremely prudish and morally strict. In fact, this is where we get the word Puritanical, which references their work ethic in addition to their disdain of pleasure.

The New World


First Settlements
    year - Plymouth Rock

Plymouth, Massachusetts: Established in 1620 by Separatists.
(Plymouth was the first successful permanent English settlement in New England)

Salem, Massachusetts: Founded in 1626 by Puritans

Boston, Massachusetts: Founded in 1630 by Puritans seeking religious freedom

Hartford, Connecticut: Settled in 1636 by Puritans from Massachusetts

New Haven, Connecticut: Founded in 1638 by a group of Separatists


Harvard University: Founded in 1636 by Puritans
Yale University: Established in 1701 by Congregationalists (a later iteration of Puritanism)
